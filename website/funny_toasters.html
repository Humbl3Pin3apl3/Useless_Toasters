How does a toaster work?

A toaster works by using electricity to heat nichrome wires, which glow red and emit infrared radiation to toast bread, while a mechanical/electrical timer system holds the bread down and pops it up when done, relying on the Maillard reaction for browning and flavor. When you press the lever, it completes an electrical circuit, activating the heating elements; a timer (often a bimetallic strip or electronic circuit) controls the duration, then releases a spring-loaded tray, ejecting the toast.
